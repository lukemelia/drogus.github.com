I'm Piotr Sarnacki

You could heard about me because of Ruby Summer of Code. I was working on mountable applications project.

Why me?

I'm a passionate programmer with great dose of curiosity and urge for challenges. I've been working commercially with ruby and rails for about 4 years now. Not only I have experience in writing web applications using rails, but also I know rails internals very well, thanks to my work during Ruby Summer of Code. At the moment I have 251 commits in rails master branch:

[ ~/rails ]$ git shortlog -s --author="Sarnacki" | grep "Sarnacki"
251  Piotr Sarnacki

Until now I worked mainly as a freelancer. It taught me developing web apps as a whole, from the beginning to the end. It also taught self organization, which is needed, if you're not bounded by 9-5 office work.

Besides working with ruby, I feel comfortable with Javascript, HTML and CSS. Although I'm not a good designer, I can implement esthetic user interfaces.

I develop applications using TDD approach and I have broad experience with integration tests, including usage of browser based tools like selenium or watir.

If there is a need I can get to lower levels of abstraction and dive into advanced SQL or write ruby C extensions (or an apache module).

I'm not a native speaker, but I think I have good communication skills and I feel that language will not be a barrier here.

Why do I want to work for you?

First of all, your attitude is great and I think that you're one of the best companies to work for. It's also very important, that you actually have fun running the company and don't want to sell it. That's why I care for that job and I don't think about it as temporary one.

I like learning and I know that I can learn a lot from you.

I read both Rework and Getting Real and I agree with ideas gathered there. It must be great to work for a company that teaches work philosophy rather than tries to adopt one.

My weaknesses

No one is perfect and I think that you should know about my weaknesses.

I lack self confidence. I fight it by leaving my comfort zone as often as I can, but I feel that I could achieve much more after getting rid of that completely.

Although I try to be pragmatic and use my time as well as I can, sometimes I can't help it and I spend half of the night trying to debug some problem that I could deal with the other way or solve it next day in the morning with clear mind.

I'm also aware that my English is not perfect, but I constantly try to learn and extend my vocabulary.
